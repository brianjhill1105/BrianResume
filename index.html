How I Work

These are the principles I live by — the rules I hold myself and my teams to every single day. Click any rule to learn more.

01

Deliver 100%, 100% of the time

There's no "good enough." Every project, every deliverable, every interaction gets my full effort. Whether it's a client website or an internal system update, I treat it like it's the most important thing on my plate — because to someone, it is. Half-measures create twice the work.

02

Complacency kills

The moment you stop pushing to improve is the moment you start falling behind. I never assume the way we've always done it is the best way. Technology moves fast, and so should we. Staying comfortable is the biggest risk.

03

Be a leader. Take care of our mission and our users always

Leadership isn't a title — it's a behavior. Every decision I make comes back to one question: does this serve the people who depend on us? In web production, that means real clients and their customers. The mission and the user experience always come first.

04

The highest level of integrity is expected — when in doubt, ask

Do the right thing, even when no one is watching. And if you're not sure what the right thing is? Ask. There's no shame in seeking clarity — the only mistake is guessing when you don't have to. Transparency builds trust.

05

Always tell the truth. We want to hear the bad news sooner than later

Problems don't age well. A small issue today becomes a crisis tomorrow if you hide it. I create environments where people feel safe bringing problems forward early, because that's when they're easiest to fix. Honesty isn't just a value — it's a strategy.

06

Understand the business, not just the technology

Technology is a tool, not the goal. I always start by understanding what the business needs to accomplish, then figure out how tech can get us there. A perfectly configured system that doesn't serve the business is just an expensive hobby.

07

Have a well-founded opinion

Don't just go with the flow — do the research, weigh the options, and bring a point of view to the table. But "well-founded" is the key word. Opinions backed by experience and data move teams forward. Opinions backed by ego hold them back.

08

Be professional in your style, speech, and follow-up

Details matter. How you present yourself, how you communicate, and whether you follow through — these things define your reputation. Professionalism isn't about being stiff; it's about being reliable, respectful, and intentional in everything you do.

09

See the world through the eyes of our users

Empathy is a superpower in tech. Whether building a website or deploying a system, I always ask: what does this feel like for the person using it? A decade at Apple drilled this into me — the best technology disappears into the experience.

10

Finish the back of the drawer

A Steve Jobs philosophy that stuck with me. Even the parts no one will see should be done right. Quality isn't just what's visible — it's the craftsmanship in every detail. If the code behind a website is messy, it'll show eventually. Do it right, all the way through.

11

Everyone sweeps the floor

No task is beneath anyone. I've been a Director and I've crawled under desks to run cables. Titles don't excuse you from the work that needs doing. The best teams are built when leaders roll up their sleeves alongside everyone else.

12

Don't take yourself too seriously. Have fun — otherwise it's not worth it

Work hard, yes — but enjoy what you do. The best creative work comes from teams that laugh together, celebrate wins, and don't let the pressure steal the joy. Life's too short for a job that doesn't light you up.
